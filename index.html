⭐ Featured · MyCAMS

When a 3.7 Rating Told Me We Had a Trust Problem, Not a UX Problem

MyCAMS · B2C Platform · 70L Users

Key Insight
"Every product has two versions — the one we think we built, and the one users actually experience. Our job is to close that gap, every single day."

MyCAMS was sitting at a 3.7 Play Store rating and slipping. Behind every complaint was one simple truth: users don't care about internal constraints or legacy bottlenecks. They only see friction. We rebuilt onboarding, fixed silent API failures, rewrote compliance-heavy instructions in human language — no big bang, just consistent 1% improvements and a lot of listening.

⭐ 3.7 → 4.5 Rating ↓ Support Queries ↑ Completion Rates Users trusted the app again

"Users don't call because something is broken. They call because they're uncertain. Fix understanding, and metrics follow."

The Lesson

In regulated fintech, UX isn't about removing steps. It's about removing uncertainty. When users feel supported — not tested — even the most complex journey feels simple.

🔥 Crisis Leadership · AMFI

7 Days. A Migration Gone Wrong. And the Mess Nobody Outside the Room Ever Saw.

AMFI Platform · B2B · 5L Distributors

Key Insight
"A product manager's real success isn't visible in the demos. It's visible in the problems no one outside the room ever finds out about."

Hours after go-live, we found something that froze the room: a large set of pending transactions hadn't migrated. For the next 7 days — literally day and night — we rebuilt, reconstructed, and validated every transaction manually. The effects lasted six months. But distributors never felt the pain. The migration is still remembered as "smooth."

✅ Zero distributor disruption 7 days of silent firefighting System fully stabilised

"The hardest place to be is 80% confidence. Sometimes the remaining uncertainty is historical, not technical — and you have to make a conscious call."

The Lesson

Product leadership isn't always about eliminating risk. Sometimes it's about knowing when you understand enough to move forward — and owning whatever comes next.

🧠 Systems Thinking · AMFI

When the Old System Knew More Than We Did

AMFI Platform · Database Migration · 1,000 → 130 Tables

Key Insight
"Legacy systems don't just store data. They store forgotten decisions. The most dangerous thing you can do is assume you fully understand them."

We built a clean new system. Then started migration. That's when we found something uncomfortable: the old system knew more than we did. Records no one could explain. Nulls where keys were mandatory. Duplicate records. After multiple dry runs stuck at 80% confidence, I told the CPO: "The last 20% won't disappear with more dry runs. We're ready to handle it in production."

1,000 → 130 DB tables 50% storage reduction 50% faster product lifecycle

"Requirements gathered from people's memories will always miss what the system quietly learned over years. Migrate with humility."

The Lesson

Waiting longer wouldn't reveal missing context. The remaining uncertainty was historical, not technical. That's when product leadership means choosing to move forward consciously.

🤝 Stakeholder Alignment · AMFI

When Every Team Pulled in a Different Direction

AMFI Platform · CAMS · Cross-functional Leadership

Key Insight
"Products slow down when teams fight for their priorities. They speed up when teams share the same purpose. Alignment isn't documentation — it's leadership."

When we started building the new AMFI platform, I expected the technical complexity. What I didn't expect was five different teams — Ops, Risk, Business, CXOs, Engineering — each pulling the product in a different direction. Everyone had a point. Everyone had pressure. And for a while, the product felt stuck. Not because we couldn't build, but because we couldn't decide what to build first.

I mapped everything: what users cared about, what regulators mandated, what ops struggled with, what CXOs needed. A pattern emerged — a few high-impact improvements could solve everyone's pain, but each team viewed them through a different lens. So I set up cross-team workshops where Ops explained bottlenecks, Risk explained controls, Engineering showed constraints. Slowly, teams started seeing each other's realities.

TAT: 7 days → 1 day Rejections: 20% → 1% Queries: 1,000 → 200/day Teams aligned

"Disagreements turned into discussions. Discussions turned into decisions. Decisions turned into impact."

The Lesson

The real challenge wasn't execution — it was alignment. Products don't slow down because of technical debt. They slow down because teams haven't agreed on what matters.

⚙️ Technical Decision · MyCAMS

The Day I Had to Tell Leadership "No" on Native

MyCAMS · Architecture Decision · Hybrid vs Native

Key Insight
"Leadership isn't about avoiding disagreement. It's about having the conviction — and data — to stand your ground when it matters."

Leadership strongly preferred native — it sounded reliable, industry-standard, "future-ready." But in our case, choosing native meant two separate codebases, double the engineering bandwidth, double the testing effort, and longer release cycles for even small UI changes. The problem we were solving wasn't about performance — it was about velocity.

I put together the case: side-by-side dev effort comparison, projected maintenance cost, roadmap velocity impact, risk analysis for multi-platform changes. Challenging leadership is never comfortable. But eventually, everyone aligned. We built on hybrid — and the results validated the decision immediately.

Dev time cut 40% GTM velocity doubled Unified UI across platforms Lower engineering overhead

"We built for scalability, not sentiment. That's the only way a product decision should be made."

The Lesson

A product team doesn't become fast by working harder. It becomes fast by fixing the foundation that slows it down — even when that means pushing back on leadership.

📋 Process Design · AMFI

The 20% Rejection Problem No One Wanted to Touch

AMFI Platform · Distributor Onboarding · Validation Design

Key Insight
"People don't fail processes. Processes fail people. Build a journey that protects users from errors, and your product becomes trusted."

During the AMFI revamp, one number bothered me more than anything: 20% of applications were getting rejected. One in five. Not because users didn't want to comply — but because the process made it far too easy to make mistakes. Missing documents, wrong file formats, incorrect naming conventions, fields filled in the wrong sequence.

The system wasn't rejecting applications. The system was enabling mistakes. So we flipped the workflow. Instead of detecting errors at the end, we designed the flow to prevent errors at the beginning — better field validations, clearer instructions, standardised naming conventions, early-stage checks, conditional logic to guide distributors step-by-step.

Rejections: 20% → 1% Cleaner case processing Less manual intervention Distributors gained confidence

"Suddenly, distributors weren't 'making mistakes' anymore — they were being guided."

The Lesson

High rejection rates are rarely a user problem. They're almost always a design problem. If your flow lets errors through, fix the flow — not the user.

📞 Support & Empathy · AMFI

Why 1,000 Customers Were Calling Every Day

AMFI Platform · Customer Support · Communication Design

Key Insight
"Most customer queries aren't caused by wrong features. They're caused by unclear experiences. When users understand the journey, they stop asking for help."

When we launched the new AMFI platform, one number haunted every review: 1,000+ customer queries per day. The flows were working. Validations were solid. Logic was functioning. So why were so many people still calling? The first instinct was to fix the product. But then I realised: people don't always call because something is broken. They call because they're uncertain.

I spent time listening to queries one by one. The pattern was clear — users didn't know which documents were acceptable, couldn't track where their application was stuck, and instructions felt ambiguous. Nothing was technically wrong. But everything felt unclear. So instead of fixing bugs, we fixed understanding — rewrote instructions in plain language, added stage-level visibility, reduced points of ambiguity.

Queries: 1,000 → 200/day Support teams freed up Ops became faster Platform felt "easy"

"We didn't change a single regulatory rule. We just made the journey feel less like guesswork."

The Lesson

Confusion is not a user problem — it's a design debt. When users feel guided, they stop calling. Clarity is the cheapest support ticket you'll ever close.

👥 People Leadership · CAMS

Growing Product Managers in the Middle of Chaos

CAMS · Mentorship · Junior PM Development

Key Insight
"Features come and go. Systems get replaced. But people you invest in carry that growth through their entire careers."

Two junior PMs — both new, both talented, both unsure where to begin. And the timing couldn't have been worse: priorities shifting weekly, Ops needing changes "yesterday," Engineering overloaded, and AMFI + MyCAMS in the middle of major transformations. I decided on one thing: if they succeed, the product succeeds.

We started with the basics — how to break down requirements, write clear PRDs, talk to engineering in their language, prioritise when everything looks important. But the bigger challenge was a chaotic environment where everyone kept redefining priorities. So I built a simple alignment framework: weekly priority-setting, shared definition of "critical," clear ownership boundaries, and a communication rhythm. Slowly, the chaos became manageable.

2 independent PMs developed Both lead meetings confidently Skyrocketed delivery speed Teams trust their decisions

"They started saying 'no' when needed — the real sign of PM maturity."

The Lesson

Building products is hard. Building product managers is harder — and infinitely more meaningful. The best investment a senior PM can make is in the people around them.

🔧 Ops Automation · AMFI

Automating the Workflows That Were Quietly Slowing Us Down

AMFI Platform · Workflow Automation · Ops Efficiency

Key Insight
"Great products don't just improve the front-end experience. They improve the organisational experience. The biggest wins happen before the user even touches the screen."

Across AMFI, we had workflows that looked straightforward on paper but were extremely painful in reality — AMFI application validations, report generation, reconciliation. Each was built years ago, expanded over time, patched repeatedly. On the outside, they "worked." On the inside, they were slowing everyone down. Distributors uploaded files in formats they preferred. Reconciliation was happening in Excel. Reports were assembled manually.

I mapped each workflow end-to-end — where does data originate, which steps depend on people instead of logic, why do rejections keep happening at the same spots. Very quickly one thing became clear: these weren't "process problems." They were "this is how we've always done it" problems. So we rebuilt them piece by piece — system-level validations, standardised formats, automated reconciliation, real-time report generation.

Ops effort ↓35% TAT: 6 days → 1 day Accuracy increased Processes became scalable

"Workflows that once felt heavy became… quiet. That's the sound of a well-automated system."

The Lesson

A workflow may be invisible to users, but its inefficiencies affect everyone — from support to engineering to business. Fixing internal processes is product work too.

🛠️ Backend Reliability · MyCAMS

Fixing the Failures Users Never See

MyCAMS · API Reliability · Silent Error Resolution

Key Insight
"Users don't see API failures. They only feel the frustration they create. A good interface makes products usable. A reliable backend makes them trustworthy. Trust is the real UX."

One of the earliest problems I faced in MyCAMS wasn't visible in the UI. The screens looked fine. Nothing seemed broken. But users were still getting stuck — "Try again," "It's not loading," "Something went wrong," no error message but it won't proceed. The problem wasn't the interface. It was the engine behind it. So I went where most PMs rarely go first: the logs.

Tiny failures. Silent failures. Validations that didn't fire. Downstream APIs returning unexpected payloads. Third-party systems taking 4–6 seconds. Retries looping but not resolving. Old functional logic written for constraints that no longer existed. Individually small. Together, slowing down the entire product. We rewrote functional validations, optimised retry logic, cleaned payload structures, added missing fallback states, streamlined error handling.

API failure rate ↓ sharply Core journey success ↑ "Mystery issues" resolved Play Store sentiment positive

"The app finally 'felt' stable — consistently. That feeling is worth more than any feature launch."

The Lesson

Backend reliability is invisible until it breaks. PMs who only look at the UI are missing half the product. Logs, retry patterns, and API payloads are product problems too.

🌐 Industry Perspective · FinTech

Why Fintech Will Never Feel Like Swiggy — And Why That's Okay

MyCAMS & AMFI · Regulated UX · FinTech Philosophy

Key Insight
"E-commerce delivers convenience. Fintech delivers confidence. Confidence always takes a few more steps — but it's worth every one of them."

One question keeps coming up: "Why can't mutual fund apps be as smooth as Swiggy?" Wrong name on Swiggy? Delivery boy calls. App glitch? Retry. Most errors are reversible. In mutual fund investing — wrong PAN? Hard stop. Name mismatch? Stop. Incorrect bank account? Stop. Not because we want to slow users, but because SEBI, AMCs, KRAs, banks all demand 100% accuracy. You can refund a pizza. You can't refund an incorrect KYC.

In MyCAMS onboarding, I couldn't remove steps, skip validations, or soften compliance. But I could simplify how the user felt while going through them. We front-loaded clarity, rewrote instructions, reduced confusion, designed for third-party failures, removed cognitive overload. We didn't copy Swiggy's UI. We copied Swiggy's intention: clarity and confidence.

Rating: 3.7 → 4.5 Onboarding completion ↑ Support queries ↓ Play Store complaints ↓

"Trust is your real conversion metric in fintech. Not speed, not beauty — trust."

The Lesson

You cannot simplify regulation — only the experience of regulation. Guide users before errors occur. Transparency beats speed. Design for confidence, not convenience.

💡 Product Innovation · MyCAMS

Replacing Uploads With Intelligence: The Account Aggregator Story

MyCAMS · Account Aggregator · Bank Mandate Digitisation

Key Insight
"Innovation in fintech is rarely about adding new features. Sometimes it's about removing old friction using the intelligence the ecosystem already offers."

The Change of Bank Mandate process was one of the most painful journeys in mutual fund servicing. Upload a cancelled cheque. Wait for manual verification. Fix rejections. Upload again. Wait again. From a user's point of view: bureaucracy. From an ops point of view: never-ending manual work. The question became: how do you simplify a process that is mandatory, sensitive, and traditionally manual?

The answer: Account Aggregator. Instead of users uploading cheques, the system could directly fetch verified bank account data from the user's financial ecosystem. But implementing it wasn't straightforward — we had to redesign the consent flow, handle partial consents, map bank codes across AMCs, standardise validations, ensure fallback journeys. Once live, users no longer uploaded anything. Ops no longer squinted at cheque images.

Manual verification → minutes 80% transactions digitised 50K+ txns/month automated Rejections dropped sharply

"Users called it 'simple.' Behind the scenes, it was anything but. That's the real craft of product."

The Lesson

The best fintech innovations don't add complexity — they use existing ecosystem intelligence to remove it. Sometimes the hardest engineering enables the simplest experience.
